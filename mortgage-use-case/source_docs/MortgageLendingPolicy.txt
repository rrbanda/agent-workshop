Version 3.2 | Effective Date: January 1, 2026

========================================
SECTION 1: LOAN PROGRAMS AND ELIGIBILITY
========================================

1.1 Conventional Loans

Conventional loans are not insured by any government agency. NovaCrest offers conventional loans with the following requirements:

- Minimum credit score: 620
- Maximum debt-to-income ratio (DTI): 43%
- Minimum down payment: 5% (PMI required below 20%)
- Maximum loan amount: $647,200 (conforming limit)

1.2 FHA Loans

Federal Housing Administration (FHA) loans are government-insured and designed for borrowers who may not qualify for conventional financing.

- Minimum credit score: 580 (with 3.5% down payment)
- Minimum credit score: 500 (with 10% down payment)
- Maximum debt-to-income ratio (DTI): 50%
- Minimum down payment: 3.5%
- Mortgage Insurance Premium (MIP) is required for the life of the loan

1.3 VA Loans

VA loans are guaranteed by the Department of Veterans Affairs and available to eligible veterans, active-duty service members, and surviving spouses.

- Minimum credit score: 620
- Maximum debt-to-income ratio (DTI): 41% (may be exceeded with compensating factors)
- No down payment required
- No private mortgage insurance required
- VA funding fee applies unless exempt

1.4 Jumbo Loans

Jumbo loans exceed conforming loan limits and carry stricter requirements.

- Minimum credit score: 700
- Maximum debt-to-income ratio (DTI): 38%
- Minimum down payment: 10%
- Additional reserve requirements: 6 months of payments in liquid assets

SECTION 2: REQUIRED DOCUMENTATION BY LOAN TYPE
============================================

2.1 Documentation Required for ALL Loan Types

The following documents are required regardless of loan type:

a) Income Verification:
   - W-2 forms: Most recent year (current or prior tax year)
   - Pay stubs: From the last 30 days, showing year-to-date earnings
   - Bank statements: Last 2 months (within 60 days of application date), all pages including blank pages

b) Property Documentation:
   - Property appraisal: Independent appraisal conducted by a licensed appraiser, completed within 120 days of closing

2.2 Additional Documentation for FHA Loans

In addition to the standard documentation above, FHA loans require:
   - Federal tax returns: Most recent 2 years (1040 with all schedules)
   - Employment verification letter from current employer

2.3 Additional Documentation for VA Loans

In addition to the standard documentation above, VA loans require:
   - DD-214 (Certificate of Release or Discharge from Active Duty)
   - VA Certificate of Eligibility (COE)
   - If active duty: Statement of Service from commanding officer

2.4 Additional Documentation for Jumbo Loans

In addition to the standard documentation above, Jumbo loans require:
   - Federal tax returns: Most recent 2 years
   - Business tax returns (if self-employed): Most recent 2 years
   - Proof of additional liquid assets (investment statements, retirement accounts)

=============================================
SECTION 3: DOCUMENT ACCEPTANCE CRITERIA
=============================================

3.1 Bank Statements

- Must be dated within 60 days of the application date
- Must show the account holder's name matching the borrower
- Must include all pages, even blank pages
- Must show the financial institution's name and logo
- Statements older than 60 days will be REJECTED
- Screenshots or partial statements will be REJECTED

3.2 W-2 Forms

- Must be from the current or immediately prior tax year
- Must show employer name, employee name, and employer identification number (EIN)
- Must show total wages and tax withholdings
- Handwritten or altered W-2 forms will be REJECTED

3.3 Pay Stubs

- Must be dated within 30 days of the application date
- Must show employer name, employee name, pay period, and year-to-date earnings
- Must show gross pay, deductions, and net pay
- Pay stubs older than 30 days will be REJECTED

3.4 Tax Returns

- Must be signed copies or IRS transcripts
- Must include all schedules and attachments
- Must be from the most recent 2 tax years
- Unsigned or incomplete returns will be REJECTED

3.5 Property Appraisal

- Must be conducted by a state-licensed or certified appraiser
- Must be completed within 120 days of the anticipated closing date
- Must include interior and exterior photographs
- Must include comparable sales analysis
- Desktop or drive-by appraisals are not acceptable for loans over $400,000

3.6 DD-214 (VA Loans)

- Must show character of discharge (Honorable or General Under Honorable Conditions)
- Must show dates of service
- Dishonorable discharge renders the applicant ineligible

3.7 VA Certificate of Eligibility

- Must be current (issued within the last 12 months or retrieved electronically)
- Must show available entitlement

SECTION 4: CONDITIONAL APPROVAL PROCESS
=============================================

4.1 Issuing Conditional Approval

When an underwriter determines that an application meets basic qualification criteria but is missing required documentation, a Conditional Approval is issued. This means:

- The borrower is approved pending satisfaction of specific conditions
- Each condition specifies exactly what document or information is needed
- Conditions must reference the specific document type from Section 2
- The borrower is notified of all conditions simultaneously

4.2 Condition Resolution Process

For each outstanding condition:
1. The borrower is notified of the specific requirement
2. The borrower uploads the requested document
3. The document is reviewed against the acceptance criteria in Section 3
4. If the document meets criteria: the condition is marked SATISFIED
5. If the document does not meet criteria: the condition remains OPEN and the borrower is notified with a specific explanation of what was wrong and what they need to provide instead

4.3 Condition Resolution Timeline

- Borrowers have 30 calendar days from conditional approval to satisfy all conditions
- Extensions may be granted on a case-by-case basis
- If conditions are not satisfied within 60 days, the application is suspended

4.4 Moving from Conditional Approval to Full Approval

An application moves from CONDITIONAL_APPROVAL to APPROVED when:
- ALL conditions have a status of SATISFIED or WAIVED
- No OPEN or PENDING_REVIEW conditions remain
- The underwriter performs a final review

SECTION 5: CREDIT AND INCOME REQUIREMENTS
=============================================

5.1 Credit Score Evaluation

- The qualifying credit score is the middle score when three bureau scores are available
- When only two bureau scores are available, the lower score is used
- Recent derogatory marks (within 12 months) require additional explanation

5.2 Debt-to-Income Ratio Calculation

DTI = (Total Monthly Debt Obligations / Gross Monthly Income) x 100

Monthly debt obligations include:
- Proposed housing payment (principal, interest, taxes, insurance)
- Minimum payments on revolving credit (credit cards)
- Installment loan payments (auto, student, personal loans)
- Alimony or child support obligations
- Other recurring obligations

5.3 Income Calculation

- Salaried employees: Base salary plus average of last 2 years' bonuses/overtime
- Hourly employees: Average hours x hourly rate, verified by 2 years of history
- Self-employed: Average of last 2 years' net income from tax returns
- Rental income: 75% of gross rental income (to account for vacancies/expenses)

=============================================
SECTION 6: PROPERTY REQUIREMENTS
=============================================

6.1 Appraisal Requirements

- The appraised value must meet or exceed the purchase price
- If the appraised value is below the purchase price, options include: renegotiate price, increase down payment, or dispute the appraisal
- The appraisal must identify any health and safety concerns

6.2 Title Requirements

- Clear title with no outstanding liens (except existing mortgage being paid off)
- Title insurance must be obtained
- Survey may be required depending on property type and location
